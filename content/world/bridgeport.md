# Bridgeport

Bridgeport is a freehold city of approximately 8,000 souls situated at the mouth of the Godsfall 
River where it meets Pell's Harbor, on the western coast of Algar. It is a city of commerce, 
ambition, and barely contained chaos — a place where fortunes are made and lost, where every 
faction has a foothold, and where the streets themselves seem to have opinions.

## Governance

Bridgeport is governed by the **Council of Trades**, a body of merchant representatives whose 
authority derives from coin rather than crown. The current High Convenor is **Seraphina Velcross**, 
a sharp political mind who maintains an uneasy balance between the city's many competing interests.

The Crown maintains a garrison at **Fort Royal**, commanded by **Colonel Alastair Thorneveil**. 
The garrison's presence is a reminder that Bridgeport's independence has limits — though most 
days, the Colonel and the Council maintain a careful mutual tolerance.

## Districts & Landmarks

### Saltstone Square
The beating heart of Bridgeport's street life, Saltstone Square is a crowded market district 
where merchants, laborers, and trouble all congregate. The **Strangled Goat** tavern sits on 
its edge — a reliable place for a drink, a rumor, or an ill-advised conversation.

### Riverbend Mills
The industrial district along Stonewash Creek, where the city's grain is ground and its goods 
are processed. Not glamorous, but essential.

### Northwood Stands
The dense woodland that borders Bridgeport to the north. Few go in without good reason, and 
not all who do come back.

## Faith

Three temples serve Bridgeport's spiritual needs — or at least its official ones:

- **Temple of Radiant Light** — devoted to Aard
- **Shrine of Iron Oath** — devoted to Kelleth  
- **Umbral House** — devoted to Noctis

## Atmosphere

Bridgeport smells like salt, fish, and ambition. Its streets are loud, its politics are 
complicated, and its people are pragmatic above all else. It is not a safe city, but it is 
an interesting one — and for those with the nerve to navigate it, full of opportunity.
